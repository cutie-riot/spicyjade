from the summer i smoked the cursed weed

I woke up from a strange dream.
I was in an offshore prison getting the brakes beaten off of me.
The weight on my body twitched, and nuzzled closer.
I opened my eyes and there they were.
Curled up upon my chest. Snoring softly.
Sometime in the night we had moved from the floor to my bed.
Nobody had been this close to me in years.
I studied Perseid's face.

Pure white fur marred with grey splotches near the eyes, and a pink nose. The silver fur around their lips enticed me.
There was no way I could've fallen back asleep under those circumstances.
I extracted myself from under them.
My back felt twisted out of shape.
There were bruises on my wrists, and my clothes were dirty from running through alleys.
I took a shower.
The paw print stamp on my hand bled from the lukewarm water running down my body, and circled down the drain.
As I dried myself off, I caught my reflection in the mirror. I seemed tired, and not just from yesterday. I looked beat down.
Brown eyes, almost black, searched over my every feature.
Dark circles were etched under my eyes. I've had trouble sleeping since I was young.

I'm a normal man, not guilty of any crimes.
I thought hard about that.
I ran a brush through my thick curly hair. The tangles hurt to get out.

In the other room, Perseid slept without waking.
Last night wasn't all dream, I thought absentmindedly as I left the bathroom.
I crept over to the table in the middle of the room.
The Spicy Jade sat unceremoniously in the middle of the table.
My usual morning routine consisted of smoking a fat bowl before doing anything.
Today, I hesitated. I thought of last nights fiasco.
I heard Perseid stirring awake just as I considered loading up the bowl anyways.

"G'morning" they said drowsily, before rolling over and falling back asleep.
That make me laugh.
I packed the bowl and sparked it.
Then I drew a long hit and blew the purple smoke directly in their sleeping face.
Perseid rose from their slumber like a cartoon dog smelling a fresh pie in a windowsill.
"Oh my god yes that smells amazing."
They sniffed the air, drawing in the pot smoke through their nostrils.
We sat and smoked in silence.
I felt the Spicy Jade weighing down my limbs and slowing down my motor functions.

"Hey No-el," Perseid jangled the handcuff encircling their wrist in my face.
"Any chance you know how to get one of these off?"
I gave them a tight lipped smile.
"Actually, I do. Hold on let me get my tools." I rose up and fumbled through my desk drawer.
I found what I was looking for. My lockpicking set.
"This might take me a few minutes."

I tinkered with the lock for a while, stopping only to take occasional bong hits.
Perseid's eyes studied the subtle movements of my hands, watching me as I tried to defeat the double locking mechanism.
"Where did you learn to do this?" Perseid asked me, the light of curiosity bright in their eyes.
"I always wanted to learn how to pick locks!"
I said nothing for while, focused on the task at hand.
With an audible click, the handcuff swung open.
"My dad taught me."
I put the cuffs on my desk. Good practice for later.

Perseid rubbed the spot where the metal was digging into their fur.
"That's so cool. What is he like, a locksmith?"
Talking about him made me feel like I was a little kid again.
Yet for some reason, I trusted them.

"No. He was a criminal." I said uncomfortably.
"Oh." They stammered, clearly caught off guard. "That's honestly pretty cool too! Really!"
I simply said nothing.
Awkwardness filled the room like a thick smoke, and I couldn't breathe.
The tension was cut by the chiming of my phone.
"Oh that's me." I scrambled to the noise. It was under my bedsheets.
69 missed calls from my coworker Trevan. Three texts.

where r u?

wake tf up

barbie had to come in, she is on the warpath.

I was late. Way late. I felt myself beginning to shift into a weed anxiety spiral.
Perseid was watching me, absorbing my mood. Their tail was tucked between their legs.
Fuck it, I thought. I sent a generic I am sick message.
No response.
I stashed the Spicy Jade into my bag, and held out my hand to Perseid
"Hey, let's head back over to Dogstack and figure this all out." They smiled and took it.

___

"This can't be right." said Perseid with extreme confusion.
We searched all afternoon, trying to retrace the anxious path i took the night before. With some luck, we found the access road off the side of the highway.
The whole house was gone.
In it's place there was one of those hideous micro apartment buildings. The sign on the gate read Calm Seas Suites.
I'd heard of the place.
There were six other building that looked just like it all over Westcoastville.
The rooms here were even smaller than mine. A lot smaller.

"Shit." I muttered, at a loss for words.
I had the crazy thought that this was because of last night, but all traces of Dogstack were gone.
Like it was never there.
"Are we sure this is the right place?" I said while peering over the gate.
There was a numerical lock on it. No picking this one.

"Hm." They sniffed the air, and a bush nearby.
"Nah. Actually, yes. Wait...I dunno. I guess yes, but something is different.
I feel as if the earth and air here are similar. My friends are not here though, and have never been here."
They sniffed the bush again.
"Other dogs live here though, but something is wrong with them. They are dumb."

I eyed the bush skeptically. "You got all that from a bush?"

"HEY. This sniffer has never been wrong! I could sniff you out of a lineup blindfolded." Perseid exclaimed, clearly offended.
They crossed their arms and glared at me.

I really looked at them.
They were wearing the same clothes from the previous night.
An asymmetric shirt, buttoned all the way to the top. Their pants were a lovely color of forest green.
Incredible style.
Not like the endless gorpcore tech bros or granola yogis that lived in this town.
It occurred to me that I really didn't know anything about them.
The only thing I knew was that they loved weed.

"Hey...sorry I didn't mean to dismiss your uh skills." I produced the Spicy Jade from my pocket. "Do you wanna smoke about it?"
Perseid's face lit up.
As we walked back up the access road toward College District, we each rolled one joint.
Rolling a joint while on the move is a high level maneuver, and they did it no problem.
I was impressed.

"Where are you from?" I asked.
"Dog Beach! I lived in a sweet pad right downtown but I got priced out."
"I've never heard of that place."
"What!? Dog Beach is a major city!"

"What do you do?"
"I want to be a graphic designer, but I'm in between jobs at the moment."
A pout began forming on their face at the thought.

"Do you know what I am? Ever seen a person like me? A human being?"
"A human bean?" They shot me a quizzical look.
"I thought you were some kind of hairless dog. I didn't wanna be rude."
"Does this mean your from a different dimension or something?"
"Wow that's a crazy thought. I'll figure it out. Or not. Either way I guess I'm stuck here for a while."

We paused underneath a coniferous tree. The smoke from our joints drifted lazily upwards,
into the little noses of a flock of sparrows. Their happy chirping suddenly stopped.

"How do you think this happened?"
We found ourselves down by Raven Park, and planted ourselves on a park bench.
Perseid pushed their glasses up and raised the joint to the sun.
"Do you think if I left this here long enough, it would ignite?"
They laughed and let out an exhale of smoke.
"The sun is hot enough, it's just that its so far away..."
I smiled.
"What does that have to do with the question?" I asked.
They shrugged.
"Could be something bought us together? Some kind of mystic force?"
Perseid looked up at me and smiled.

I felt a buzzing inside of my chest.
What I wanted to say was that maybe this was meant to be.
"Do you um have any questions for me?" I squeaked out.
"Yeah actually!" They looked thoughtful for a second.
"What do you do?!"
"I'm a cook..." I trailed off. "I grill barbeque."
"WHAT" exclaimed Perseid.
"What an amazing gig! Meat all day every day! Do you like it?"
"Not really." I said flatly.
"Oh. Ok how about this one? Where are YOU from!?"
I shifted uncomfortably.
"Uh next question."

"Oh." Perseid seemed put off by that.

This wasn't going well.
I desperately wanted to change the subject, but didn't know what to say.
Perseid broke the silence.
"We are friends, right?" Their eyes held mine, and they grabbed my hand.
"Of course!" I said quickly.
They leaned in close.
"Promise?"
They smelled good. Like warm vanilla.
This was the second time we had been this close.
I resisted the urge to lean in and rest my head on their chest.
"I promise."
"Then, can you do me a favor?" Perseid's eyes were pleading.
"Whatever you need."
"Are y'all hiring at the meat place?! Since I'm technically homeless now, I'll need a job.
Maybe you could also let me stay at your place for a while? At least until I get back on my feet."
I hesitated at the words.
My landlord would kill me if he figured out I had anyone else living at my place.

"Well...I bet we could figure something out with sleeping arrangements."
"Yes!" they exclaimed, pumping their fist. "And what about the meat place? Are you hiring?"
"Well..."
Our last front of house person Cody had recently passed away.
He was taking deep sea diving lessons and rose up to fast.
Before that, I never knew that the bends could be fatal.
I didn't mention this fact to them.
"Honestly, we could use help."

___

There was no honor in barbequing all day. I smelled like meat all the time.
When I first walked into Barbies Discount BBQ i just needed something easy.
Somewhere I could turn off my thoughts and not talk to anyone.
Smoking meat and weed all day wasn't a terrible gig.
Mostly I felt ambivalent about it.
I had settled into a somewhat comfortable position as kitchen manager.

Perseid and I entered the BBQ joint reeking of weed.
They sniffed the air.
"Wow it smells amazing in here! We've entered meat town!"
Their tail was wagging furiously.
Trevan was working the register, preparing to close up shop.
He was one of those rich kid indie rock guys who always looked a little disheveled, yet handsome.
Definitely didn't need this job, but refused to quit.
Trevan flashed me a smug smile, which kind of pissed me off.

"Well, if it isn't the man himself! I thought you were sick? Barbie is looking for you, dude."
Maybe it was the smell of meat permeating the restaurant, but he didn't mention the weed scent.
He stared at Perseid.
"Oh! Wow dude, freaky costume! That's actually so dope."
Perseid flashed him a warm smile and held out their paw.
"My names Perseid! Pleased to meetcha!"
I never really liked Trevan, classic front of house personality.
He had a way of talking that always seemed like flirting.

Trevan grasped Perseid's hand and held it for a little too long.
"The name is Trevan, but all the cuties call me Trev."
Perseid giggled.
I felt the hair on the back of my neck bristle.
Why did I feel so defensive of them?
"This is the new hire, so don't be weird."
I walked behind the counter, toward where Barbie's office was.
"Hey, why don't you show them around. I'll talk to Barbie."
Trevan rolled his eyes at me, but I ignored it.
To get to Barbie's office, you had to walk through the kitchen.

The kitchen had seen better days.
If you looked too closely at anything, you would see problems.
You could see all the way down to the foundations through a hole in the floor.
The grill was dirty, and hadn't been properly cleaned in a while.
The florescent bulbs buzzed with electricity, and weren't grounded properly. Sometimes they would spark up.
Everything was coated in a thin layer of grease and oil.
As the kitchen manager, it was technically my job to fix all that.
The last person with this job quit after Cody died. We all found out via a mass email from Barbie.
That was the final straw for her. They were best friends.
Francis moved to Sin City and became a black jack dealer.
Since I'm the only cook here now, nobody has complained.

I entered Barbie's office.
Barbie always looked a little ill. She had a lap band surgery a few years back, and had lost over 100 pounds.
She looked better back then. She laughed more. Now she just looked like a shell of who she was.
That didn't make me feel guilty that she had to work for me today.
Even though she owned the business, she never barbequed a day in her life.
She was sweaty. The air conditioner in the kitchen broke months ago, and I'm sure she wasn't used to working in the heat.
A large smear of grease streaked across her expensive blouse.
Smug satisfaction washed over me, because I knew that she struggled without me today.
At first, she ignored me. She was rifling around in her desk drawer for something.
She pulled a frayed piece of paper out of the drawer, then closed it.

Then she looked up, and our eyes met.
Her expression soured, and she clicked her tongue.
"Look what the cat dragged in. Or should I say the dog?
She glanced at her gaudy diamond encrusted watch.
"A little late for your shift aren't you? Who is that...person that you brought in here?"

Barbie was a habitual camera watcher. The store cameras were set up so that she could access
the footage anywhere. Six monitors right next to her desk showed her every angle of the store imaginable.
All day it was texts from her, micro managing everything we were doing.
She owned the business, and also the building.
Trevan told me that she got it from her rich family, who wanted something to keep her busy and out of their hair.

"Yeah, I hired a new person. They're a furry so that's why they look like that." I lied.
"Oh? One of them furries? I read about those on the web! I suppose it takes all types in this world." said Barbie knowingly.
"I always wanted a dog for the shop." she said dryly.

Through the CCTV I saw Trevan showing Perseid how to restock the barbeque bottles.
His hand was on their shoulder. They didn't seem to mind.

I felt a roiling inside me.
Anger?
No, this was different.
I was reminded of watching my sister have fun with her beautiful friends.
Jealousy.
"That's your fucking dog." the voice in my head screamed.

I needed some air.

"Listen Barbie, I am sick. Diarrhea." I lied.
Legally, you can't work with food if you have diarrhea.
"I only came by the show the new person around."
Barbie's face went from a look of anger to a look of disgust.
"Yeah that's fine I guess. Everything is already done for the day, anyways." she said dismissively.

I turned to leave.

"One more thing before you go."
She tapped the sheet that she placed on her desk with a ringed finger.
It read "noels call outs."
The paper was filled with tallies, more then fifty.
I looked away, not meeting her gaze
"You know my situation. What happened." I said timidly.
"My hands are tied on this, Noel." she said with exasperation. "What kind of example would I be setting if
I keep letting this go unpunished?"
She gestured pointedly at the sheet.
"I know you had your family issues but enough is enough!"
She turned her back to me, returning to the monitors.
I was dismissed.
"Figure your life out. If are so much as even late one more time, I've got to let you go."
Real anger flared inside me.
Go fuck yourself is what I wanted to say.
"I hear you. I'll try harder."

I turned and walked away before my tears started welling up,
but I knew she saw them anyway from camera 3.

I went back to the front of house to get Perseid so we could go home.
Without any luck.
Perseid and Trevan had already left.
Together.
I looked around the lobby of the store.
The close was shit.
