ch. 2 — dogstack
from the summer i smoked the cursed weed

I returned to my apartment.
Unlike my sisters place, my building was at least 80 years old.
The building manager was a decrepit old man who I avoided at all costs.
I ducked into my little studio apartment, Room 013.
The room was sparsely decorated.

A single coffee table in the middle of the room.
A small bed.
A guitar gathering dust in the corner.
No couch.

I reached into my bag and pulled out the Spicy Jade, tossing it on the coffee table.
My usual nights followed a predictable routine.
Lay on the floor, play guitar until late, get yelled at by neighbor, smoke until i forgot myself, and drift off
into fitful sleep. I'd stopped playing guitar late to appease my neighbor, and at this point I spent a lot of time laying on the floor, staring at the ceiling.
That was my whole life.
My eyes drifted from the ceiling to the Spicy Jade funking up the place.

I sidled up to the table.
"It's probably fine, right?" I said aloud to nobody.
Living alone had that effect on me.
My sister had just smoked this and nothing bad happened.
I thought back to my old next door neighbor Spaz and his tainted stash.

I found myself grinding the fragrant buds into dust rolling it into a perfect little joint.
"This is my treat." I said to myself softly.

I always loved smoking weed by myself.
Talking with other people was always difficult. Getting high alone helped me retreat inward.
No shitty cops profiling me, no normies looking at me weird, just that perfect numbness between the eyes.
For an hour or two, I could forget.

The smoke from the Spicy Jade filled my small studio.
It was then that I noticed the smoke was actually purple, it wasn't a trick of the light.
My apartment didn't have any windows.
"That can't be right. Maybe this stuff was dusted after all?" I said to no one in particular.
I took another hit.

The Spicy Jade was too loud. I immediately fell into another coughing fit.
Legends say that coughing makes you get more high, but that's just the lack of oxygen.
I remembered the old anti-weed commercials that used to come on the TV.
A stern face, judging me from across the screen.
If it isn't air, don't breathe it.
I needed to leave the apartment. All that smoke was frying my lungs.

My shoes were on before the thought fully entered my head.
I walked outside.
Summer was coming to an end. It was still warm outside, but I felt fall coming around the corner.

My anxiety propelled me. A classic weed spiral.
Rent was due and I was short a couple of hundred bucks.
My sister is smoking this stuff too I should warn her.
I have work tomorrow, it's 2am and I am wandering around town.

I walked.
Through a park and over a bridge.
Along backstreets and narrow alleys.
I stopped to rest at a bus stop to catch my breath.
My fingers reached into my pockets, searching for my phone so i could check the time. Nothing there.
Inside of my other pocket was half a stick of beef jerky.
I sighed, and pressed my face into my hands.
Why fucking me? I thought.
I began to hear it.
Faint at first, but then unmistakable.
The sounds of a DIY house show.

Why the hell not? I thought uncharacteristically.
The raucous noise led me to an unremarkable three story house off the side of the highway.
The roar from the cars whizzing past was intermingled with the sounds of the show happening inside.

My thoughts came fast one after another cascading over me.
I am high as fuck.
I am socially anxious.
I do not know these people.
This was one of my worst nightmares come to pass.
Why am I doing this to myself?
Maybe I felt more courageous because of the Spicy Jade,
Or maybe something in me still thought punk rock could save my life.
Either way, I went in.

Something was off.
The place was full of dog people.
Humanoid with cute dog faces.
Some were tall punks and some were wearing hipster glasses.
They were all really cute, and fuzzy all over.
I had already resigned that I was hallucinating, so I just went with it.
There was a golden retriever working the door.
"Um hi I've never been here before. Is it too late to catch the show?" I said awkwardly.
"Not at all!" she said cheerfully. "Last band is up right now. I think they were called Dark Harbors?"
She gestured toward a jar. It was labeled with a dollar sign.

At that moment I realized I didn't have my wallet.
I began to hear a Voice.
Not my internal monologue. Something not from my mind, yet vaguely familiar.
I couldn't place it.
It was so small, I thought I was going crazy.
Whispering directly into my brain.

"Don't you wanna party?" the small voice laughed.
"Use the jerky."

I pulled it out.
Her eyes lit up.
"For me? A little piece of meat? Ok you can come in! It's pay what you can after all!"
she marked my right hand with a little paw print stamp. "Welcome to Dogstack!"
I nodded and made my way inside. She called after me. "Thanks for the jerky! I'll remember that! My name is Goldie!"
"Oh. Hi." I said curtly, without turning around. I walked a bit faster to avoid talking to her.

Typical house show vibes. Everyone was drunk, and talking loud. Two French Poodles were taking turns smelling each other all over.
They saw me staring. I quickly walked past them towards the basement, where the band was playing.
The vocalist was hard to hear, but their music sounded good. Angry words barked to a frothing crowd.
They were chasing each other in a circle, a crush of bodies and musk.
I got caught up in the moment.
I moshed and danced around, chasing and being chased.
The band finished with a definitive howl, and the crowd joined in.
I felt the howl rise up in me and I let it out.
One with the pack.
The basement started emptying out.
This was the part of shows that I hated.
Talking and chatting with strangers was something that I wished I could do, but I always ran out of things to say.
I rushed up the stairs and bumped directly into someone.

They were short. Their face smushed into the middle of my chest.
White-grey fur dappled their body, which silvered around their mouth.
Beautiful eyes hid behind round framed glasses. My heart beat loud in my head.
I was attracted to them, even though they weren't human.
They were androgynous. Their gender was completely alien to me.
I didn't care.
All I knew is I wanted to know them.

They were sniffing me. Sniffing my pockets.
"Hey dude! What's that in there? It smells amazing!" Their tail was wagging.

"Oh yeah I had some jerky in there before...sorry I'm all out." I was talking to them, but looking at my feet.
They stamped their chic boots impatiently.
"What? No man, not that. I smell reefer."

I reached into my pocket and pulled out a perfectly rolled joint. I could smell the Spicy Jade instantly.
Was that there before? I thought to myself.
Some of the dogs near to me seemed to orbit a little bit closer, expectantly.

I smoked it up crazy with those dogs. It felt real nice to feel like I was in with them. Laughing this easily wasn't something I did often.
Weed this potent either made me forget about my social anxiety, or bumped it up by one thousand percent.
I pulled the dog with round glasses aside, into the backyard. More smokers. A pug with a goofy face and a hound dog with a saggy eyes.
"What's your name?" I asked them.
They just smiled at me. "Perseid. How about you?"
"I'm Noel."
"No-el" They repeated, nodding as if memorizing the cadence of it.
I loved the way they said my name, with two syllables instead of one.
I felt my heart skip a beat.

At that moment, I started hearing shrieking coming from inside the house.
The four of us in the yard stared inside through the back window.
The police had showed up.
A Rottweiler and a German Shepard pushed their way around, barking like crazy.
"Fuck" I said to myself softly.
"Yeah, It's a long standing compromise between us and them." Perseid's eyes darkened, their fur standing on end.

"They rob us, we are allowed to keep doing shows here." said the Pug darkly.
"At least they can't see us out here..." whimpered the Hound,
Perseid said nothing.
I watched some of the money from the door make its way into the German Shepard's pocket.
Goldie looked away and whined, careful to avoid eye contact.

Perseid was growling.
They were livid, glowering through the window.
Meanwhile, I felt frozen.
I was a stupid kid all over again. Hiding under a car while my friends got arrested. Loaded into a police van for the crime of
smoking a joint while playing street hockey.
There was that small voice again.
"Time to go."
The pace of my heart quickened, I felt like I was gonna yak.
Before I could figure out my next move, Perseid entered the house.
"Oh no" said the Pug weakly "Here we go."
"Lets get out of here!" the Hound said in a panic, already halfway over the back wall.

I really wanted to vault over that back wall with them.
To run away and never look back.
I swallowed my cowardice and followed Perseid inside.

Perseid was pushing their finger into the chest of the Rottweiler Cop.
"Where is your warrant officer? This is a private residence you shouldn't be in here."
The Doberman Cop laughed. I watched as the German Shepard Cop flank him, hand on his baton.
All of the dogs around us looked away, and gave us a wide berth.
I quickly looked at their badges.
Officer Rott and Officer Shepard.

"Well, we are here because you are causing a disturbance." Shepard smiled wide, exposing sharp teeth.
"You know all the homeless fucks in this city come back here to piss and shit."
He shot a look toward Rott.
The Rottweiler cop nodded, and grabbed Perseid's wrist.
"Looks like you just assaulted a Westcoastville Police Officer, freak."
A vicious smile spread across Rott's face as he pulled a pair of handcuffs out of his belt.

The small voice inside me was getting louder.
"LEAVE. NOW." it seemed to scream.
I looked around the room.
Angry faces. Scared faces. Dogs frozen by the fear of authority.
All but Perseid.
The only thing on their face was pure determination.
It was infectious.
There was a hierarchy here. They had guns. Bulletproof vests. Batons.
However, we had something they didn't.
Numbers.

"JUMP THEM, THEY CAN'T ARREST ALL OF US."
I felt both my feet leave the ground and plant themselves into Rott's chest.
The cop flew back, crashing into Shepard. All three of us hit the ground.
A cacophony of laughs, barks, and howls erupted around us.

Perseid scrambled away, one cuff already clicked onto their paw.
Shepard rose first, and twisted my arm behind my back.
Rott, got up next, wheezing and red in the face.

I was going to get arrested.
The dog cops began to drag me away.
Why did I do that? I thought furiously. I just ruined my life.
"Don't let them take me!" I cried out.
It was well known that the Westcoastville Police Department regularly beat down the people they arrested.
Everyone knows someone that knows someone who has been mistreated by them.
That's one thing that East Coast City had in common with this place.
Shepard raised his fist, preparing to strike me. I braced for the hit and closed my eyes.
I deserved this.

That's when I heard the growl.
A collective growl.
Goldie was the only one I recognized.
Her friendly face was pulled back into a toothy snarl.
"This is our home, and I say your trespassing. Put him down." she said through gritted teeth.
Rott and Shepard just looked at each other.
I can't remember who reached first, Rott or Shepard. They never drew their guns.
The dogs were upon them fast.
Howling, scratching, biting, screaming.
I frantically crawled out from beneath the melee and hid under the merch table.
The hiding spot was already taken.
Perseid was there, whimpering and shaken.

I grabbed their shoulder. "I think its time to go." I had to yell to be heard over the din of fighting.
I took their hand, and led them out the rear door and over the back wall.

Then we ran. The sounds of animals fighting grew quieter as we got farther away from Dogstack.
Replaced by the sounds of cars driving fast.
I don't know how, but we somehow made it back to my apartment.
We sat in silence for a long time.
"No-el...why did you do that?" Perseid gave me a hard stare.
I withered under that look.
"Look, they were gonna arrest you! I didn't want you to get hurt! I was trying to protect you!" I stammered loudly, stumbling over the words.
"Something came over me and I couldn't control myself."
Perseid shook their head.
"No, why did we run away? I left my roommate Goldie behind. I'm just worried about her."
I thought about that.
Fear? Self preservation?
Noel is a fucking coward. My friends back home used to say that to me after I hid from the cops.
Maybe that's why I couldn't answer their question.
I said nothing.
Perseid's eyes held mine, and they grabbed my hand.
"Why did you save me?"
I couldn't tell them about the sparks I felt between us. I hadn't even known them for more than four hours.
"I like you." I said cautiously, embarrassment creeping across my face.

Perseid made a face and laughed.

"Well that's unexpected. You don't even know me!" They curled up by my side.
They were right.
I didn't know them, but I wanted to.
We spent a long time like that, awakening every time sirens sounded throughout the night.
